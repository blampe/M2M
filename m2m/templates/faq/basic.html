What are the rules?

Short Answer:

  1. Don't be a jackass.

  2. Don't be a dumbass.

  3. Don't be a snitch...ass.

Long Answer:

  1. Don't be a jackass.

    You learned about this at orientation, and (surprise!) it applies for the entirety of your Mudd career, everywhere, at all times.. Ideally you'll choose to apply it afterwards, too, but it applies whether you like it or not while you're here. And you really ought to like it, unless you're a douchebag.

    You're not a douchebag, are you?

  2. Don't be a dumbass.

    Look, I'll be honest. I don't know where the files on the network come from, for sure, and I definitely don't want to know — deniability, etc., etc. — but I can certainly imagine scenarios where certain elements of the law might frown on the methods used.

    Just, be careful. Students DO get cease-and-desist letters, which the school takes seriously, and you need to be aware of that. Just because the Network is safe, does NOT mean that it is safe to behave as if the Internet-at-large is just a bigger version of it. Don't be fooled; M2M is family, the FBI — though another 3-character association — is not. At least not in the cool-uncle way that M2M is.

    Don't be stupid. I don't want bad shit to happen to you because of careless seeding.

    ...Or, uh, whatever it's called.

  3. Don't be a snitch...ass.

    Your use of the search functionality on this site implies that you do not believe its existence violates the honor code. If this is not the case, leave; it's in the terms of service, which you are required to have read before utilizing any function of this site.

    Likewise, utilizing the Network implies that you're okay with its existence. I can't make you agree to this, but Rule #1, guys; Rule #1.

    But the purpose of this rule is to make sure that nobody violates Rule #2 and starts posting all over the Internet-at-large about how great our Network is. That kind of shit gets noticed, Nancy (funwiki, I am staring daggers at you).

    Please, please, please, for the love of whatever you hold where God traditionally would be held, do not do this.

    Rule #3: The Network does not exist, has never existed, and will never exist. Behave accordingly.

What is "The Network"?

Short Answer:

A bunch of shit that you can take.

Long Answer:

The Network is the name given to the collective intellectual wealth comprised by Mudd's student run servers. It may or may not be, strictly speaking, legal, but because of the closed nature of Mudd's intranet, it's protected from outside scrutiny.

This means that once something is on the Network, it's safe to grab for yourself, whatever it might be.

Disclaimer: Rule #2

What is M2M?

Short Answer:

Your lord and savior, the gateway to paradise.

Long Answer:

M2M is a search engine, which indexes the files hosted by the servers on the Network. You can search for whatever you want, and if it's publically available, and older than about 23 hours, M2M will show you where it is. Sometimes you can directly download it from the search results page, you lucky late-comers you.

What is a server?

Short Answer:

A computer that has shit on it.

Long Answer:

Okay, so, I know they say there's no such thing as a dumb question, but if you're at this school, and you need to ask this question, then...something might have gone wrong with the admission process. I'm just saying. In any event, I'm sure there is someone within 20 feet of you who is not only able but willing to wax poetic about what it means to be a 'server' on the Network.
